Biography

Keyvan Hardani is an AI & Agentic Systems Lead Engineer and researcher based in Munich, Germany, specializing in AI Safety, Adversarial Machine Learning, and Automotive Cybersecurity. Currently working as Project Engineer for Automotive System and Integration Testing at MAN Truck & Bus SE, he leads HiL Security teams and develops AI-driven security solutions including Reinforcement Learning Firewalls and CNN-based Intrusion Detection Systems. His research focuses on adversarial trigger generation, reward model security, and real-time automotive network protection. He completed his Master of Science in Cyber Security at Hochschule der Bayerischen Wirtschaft (HDBW) Munich, with his thesis on manipulation of neural language models and defense strategies against trojaned AI systems.

German-OCR: Vision-Language Models for Document Processing

A family of fine-tuned Vision-Language Models (VLMs) specifically optimized for precise text recognition from German documents including invoices, forms, contracts, and business documents. All models output structured data in Markdown, JSON, or HTML format and are designed for privacy-first, local deployment.

  • German-OCR-Turbo - High-Speed Document OCR (Qwen3-VL-2B) [Ollama] [HuggingFace]
    3x faster than DeepSeek-OCR, ~5s/document on GPU, 100% accuracy on German invoices. 1.9GB model with 256K context window.
  • German-OCR - Precision Document OCR (Qwen2-VL-2B) [Ollama] [HuggingFace]
    Fine-tuned with QLoRA (4-bit), 125K context window, optimized for structured Markdown output. Apache 2.0 licensed.
  • German-OCR-3B - Extended Capacity Model (Qwen2.5-VL-3B) [Ollama] [HuggingFace]
    7.5GB model for complex multi-page documents and enhanced semantic understanding.
  • German-OCR-Engine - Desktop Application (Tech Preview) [GitHub] [Feedback]
    Native C++/Qt6 desktop application with encrypted model deployment, llama.cpp inference, and GPU acceleration. Privacy-first design with zero cloud dependencies.
